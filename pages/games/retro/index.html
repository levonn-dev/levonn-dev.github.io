The Retro Room

I have put together a room specifically purposed for retro gaming.

Essentially everything older than PS3 (anything not HDMI native output) goes composite RCA into a RetroTink4k through as little switches as possible which is currently a max of 2 in the chain, including the VHS that takes the NES RF in. The Genesis is using the model 1 base and cd tower of power while the model 2 battleship is stored. The model 1 base is using a custom model 1 male + stereo mini to female model 2 enabling use of stereo sound with both the 32x with the included model 2 patch cable or standard model 2 cable.

The PS3 and newer (HDMI native outputs - with the exception of the PS5, that goes direct to TV) go into an HDMI switch - both that switch and the RetroTink go to the TV through 1 switch which has an additional loose HDMI for laptops or whatever and then a final HDMI splitter which splits the output into an Avermedia capture device which goes out to USB-C intended for a laptop or whatever.

The TV has optical audio out into a Fiio DAC which feeds into the Yamaha amp outputting to Quest speakers.

As much as possible is using composite RCA and the RetroTink has lagless post-processing to simulate classic CRT visuals because I'm aiming to have everything look like I remember it from when I played it as a kid. The external capture card allows recording or streaming seamlessly of any system, which is great for capturing speed runs on original hardware or digitizing VHS.

The CRT is a Sony KV-13FM12 with the CDX through RF and classic VCR style NES through AV for best performance and classic feel in games like NES Tetris.
