About

My first computer was in 1998. It was running Windows 98, and I was constantly trying to change things and learn more about it. This is what started my interest in learning about computers and the technology that powers them. I've been the admin of my MS Windows network since I was 13, while I was using a Linux PC with SAMBA.

Games were the gateway. Before I knew what software engineering was, I was reading save files, hunting glitches, and tweaking configs for whatever I was playing. The same curiosity that made me take apart Windows turned into a career taking apart bigger systems.

I'm a Staff Software Engineer based in the Toronto area, with around fifteen years across financial and enterprise systems. I started consulting at NTT DATA, spent six years at IBM as an architect, and most recently five years at Exiger leading large scale projects including a monolith-to-microservices migration. These days I think a lot about AI-assisted developer workflows, the next chapter of how engineers actually ship software, and what that software will be capable of.

Outside of work I still build and play games. Pebble Adventure (in the Projects window) was my recent attempt at bridging an RPG onto a smart-watch screen that promotes healthier living. I still play them too: Rainbow Six, Ragnarok Online, Retro Games, the occasional UT round. The thread runs both ways. Playing games sharpens how I think about state machines and edge cases at work; shipping production systems keeps the differences between developer intended and user discovered always front of mind.
